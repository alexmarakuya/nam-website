Why Koh Phangan

Growing Demand, Limited Quality Supply

Strong and sustained demand

Koh Phangan attracts visitors year-round, driven by long-stay travelers and remote professionals. During peak months, occupancy rates regularly exceed 90%.

Source: Tourism Authority of Thailand (TAT)

Limited quality homes

The island’s inventory is polarized between low-cost bungalows and luxury resorts. There’s a clear shortage of mid-to-high-end, design-focused homes.

Source: C9 Hotelworks, 2024

Rising land prices

Land prices have risen 100–200% in the past three years, outpacing rental rate growth, making smaller, efficient ownership models more attractive.

Source: REIC, Ministry of Finance
06
Koh Phangan

The island

Hidden gem — the “alternative” Bali

Well-developed yet not overcrowded, Koh Phangan attracts people seeking a long-term base with strong community energy and a more alternative, lifestyle-oriented culture.

In the heart of Southeast Asia

20-min ride from nearby Koh Samui International Airport, with boats leaving every few hours.

Excellent infrastructure & amenities

High-speed internet, cafés, fine-dining, affordable local food, western hospitals & reliable logistics and transportation.

Pleasant climate all year-round

Tropical climate with warm weather throughout the year. Average highs of 30–33°C with a distinct dry season from January to April.
