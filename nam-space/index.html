The evolution of third places

Throughout history, the most transformative ideas weren't born in isolation. They emerged from third places — the spaces between home and work where strangers became collaborators and movements took root.

1650

The Coffeehouse

Where revolutions were sparked

The scientific revolution was born not in laboratories but in coffeehouses, where thinkers, merchants, and inventors exchanged ideas freely across disciplines.

1880s

The Artist Colony

Where movements were forged

From Montmartre to the Bauhaus, the most influential creative movements emerged from intentional communities where proximity produced what isolation never could.

1995

The Hackerspace

Open-source culture in physical form

Radical openness and collaborative making. Shared tools, shared knowledge, no gatekeepers. The movement that planted the seeds for the entire coworking era.

1990s

The Internet Cafe

The cautionary tale

Solved access to a tool, then became redundant when everyone had it at home. A reminder that spaces built around tools alone don't endure.

2005

The Coworking Space

Environment for the freelance era

Gave independent workers a home. Now facing its own inflection point as AI reshapes what it means to work, and what it means to work together.

Now

The Co-creation Space

The third place for the post-AI world

Every era had its third place — the co-creation space is ours. Built for the work that only happens when people come together to create from depth.
