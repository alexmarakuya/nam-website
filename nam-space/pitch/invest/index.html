02
The Thesis

The forest model

NāM is designed like a permaculture forest — every business feeds the others. Nothing is isolated. Every relationship is reciprocal.

Rooms fill the campus

Guests stay weekly or monthly. They eat at the café, use the co-working studio, attend programs. One booking activates multiple revenue streams.

The campus fills the rooms

A design-led co-working space, wellness, community events — these are why people book. The rooms are not just rooms. They’re the accommodation layer of a destination.

Every business compounds

Academy cohorts fill rooms. Retreats use every facility. Café traffic draws walk-ins who become members. The whole is worth dramatically more than the sum of its parts.

09
Structure

The SPV model

Each ecosystem business is a Single Purpose Vehicle — cleanly separated, independently funded, with clear terms.

The operator-investor brings

Capital to build out and equip their space

Operational expertise to run the business

Minimum investment covering full build + 12 months runway

NāM Space provides

Physical space, built and ready for fit-out

Thai legal partnership (majority stakeholder)

Brand, community, governance, and island knowledge

NāM Space holds a 10–15% revenue share per SPV (negotiated per deal). Each business pays a monthly Community Contribution to the shared maintenance fund.

Governance

Radical transparency

Every business contributes THB 49,000/month to a ring-fenced community fund. Every baht is visible.

Live dashboard

nam.space/transparency — fund balance, month-to-date income vs expenditure, updated in real time.

Monthly reports

Published on the 5th. Full income and expenditure breakdown, fund balance, plain language — no jargon.

Quarterly meetings

Open to all operators and members. Financial review, open floor, any proposed changes to contribution rates.

The fund covers cleaning, pool, landscaping, internet, utilities, repairs, security, and community programming. Surplus rolls into capital reserve. Deficit is covered by NāM Core and reported openly.
