Risk Management

Risks and how we offset them

Seasonality

Low season is offset through curated retreats, team offsites, and hacker houses that attract year-round demand. We’re also building collaborations with spaces globally — particularly in Australia, where their winter coincides with our low season, creating a natural exchange pipeline.

Local & regulatory

The founder is Thai with deep connections to local government, enabling special privileges and institutional support. This isn’t an outsider project — it’s built with the island, not on top of it.

Market demand

Koh Phangan is transitioning from party island to wellness and entrepreneur hub. The founder is aiming to work with central government to position Thailand as a premier digital nomad destination — and NāM is designed to lead that shift.

Execution

We operate like a lean startup — short feedback loops, continuous improvement, and heavy use of AI to drive efficiency across operations. The founder is personally invested and deeply motivated to grow this into something much larger.

Proof of Concept

Kin Haus — our MVP

Before building NāM Space, we launched Kin Haus — a boutique co-living space on Koh Phangan with 3 rooms. In our first two months of full operations (Feb–Mar 2026), the numbers speak for themselves.

What this proves: The co-living model works on Koh Phangan with strong margins — even as a rented property. NāM Space takes this further with owned land, purpose-built architecture, and multiple revenue streams. Both properties sit next to each other — when one is full, we refer guests to the other, creating a natural overflow pipeline.
