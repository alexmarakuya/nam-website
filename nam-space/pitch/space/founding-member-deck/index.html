02
Koh Phangan

Then I found the island

I landed on Koh Phangan and it had almost everything. The creative energy. The nature. The wellness. The events. The people building interesting things.

It was only missing one crucial component: a physical space that brings it all together. A home for founders and people building things that matter.

So I started building. First a community. Then I took an abandoned villa and turned it into Kin Haus, a co-living and co-working house. It became the proof of concept for something much bigger.

05

This is what community feels like. Friends from all over the world, connected to my Thai roots through my neighbors.

04
The deeper why

But I also saw something else

The island was struggling. People were treating it as a playground. Without respect for local culture, for nature, or for the spirit that makes Koh Phangan special.

This project doesn’t want to create another import. Another hustle incubator. I want to create something that comes from the land, from the spirit of generosity and tolerance that Thailand embodies, and fuse it with the creativity, the foresight, and the open spirit of the international community.

A place of balance. Between working and living. Between giving and receiving. Between learning and teaching. Between going inside and opening up. Between nature and technology.

Now I’m building that place.

Why here

Why Koh Phangan

Growing demand

Koh Phangan is pulling a new wave of independent professionals — founders, designers, developers — who stay for months, not days. The island lacks quality infrastructure to match the demand.

Quality gap

Most co-working on the island is improvised. Cafés with wifi, noisy shared spaces. There’s no dedicated, design-led workspace built for focused, professional work.

Rising land values

Land prices on Phangan have increased significantly year-on-year. Building now means locking in costs before the next wave of development drives prices further.

Commitments

Every system helps the island

We design every part of the campus to reduce our footprint, strengthen the local ecosystem, and share surplus with our neighbours.

Solar power

Reduce reliance on the grid and protect the space from the island’s frequent power cuts. Energy independence from day one.

Rainwater harvesting & storage

Reduce our impact on the island’s water crisis. Surplus shared with neighbours when possible.

Waste management

Full composting, zero single-use plastics, eco-friendly packaging. Working with local initiatives to close the loop.

Local & recyclable materials

Architecture sourced from local and recyclable materials wherever possible. Building with what the island already provides.

Greywater treatment

Wastewater treated and reused in the permaculture gardens. Nothing wasted, everything cycles back.

Permaculture gardens

Connecting people with the land and the local community. Growing food, learning from Thai agricultural traditions, giving back.

Exit options

You’re never locked in

Available after 5 years, with 12 months written notice.

A

Sell to another person

Find a buyer. Existing founding members vote on approval by simple majority. NāM retains right of first refusal. If no approved buyer is found within 24 months, NāM buys the unit back at the fixed appreciation price.

B

Sell back to NāM

NāM buys the unit back at original price plus 5% per year held. After 5 years on a €55,000 unit that’s €70,150 — or €82,900 on a €65,000 unit. Simple, fixed, no disputes.

C

Convert to profit share

Hand the unit to NāM operations and join the profit share arrangement. Conversion terms to be defined.
