Why here

Why Koh Phangan

Growing demand

Koh Phangan is pulling a new wave of creative professionals — founders, designers, developers — who stay for months, not days. The island lacks quality infrastructure to match the demand.

Quality gap

Most coworking on the island is improvised. Cafés with wifi, noisy shared spaces. There’s no dedicated, design-led workspace built for focused creative work.

Rising land values

Land prices on Phangan have increased significantly year-on-year. Building now means locking in costs before the next wave of development drives prices further.

06
The Island

Koh Phangan

Hidden gem turning heads

Beyond the full moon party reputation, Phangan is becoming one of Southeast Asia’s most sought-after islands for long-stay creatives.

Heart of Southeast Asia

Direct flights to Bangkok, Samui airport 30 minutes by boat. Connected enough to feel accessible, far enough to feel like an escape.

Growing infrastructure

Fast fibre internet, international schools, quality healthcare. The island is maturing rapidly while keeping its character.

Year-round tropical climate

Warm days, ocean breezes. A climate that energises rather than drains — perfect for sustained creative output.

The site

Surrounded by nature

The NāM campus sits on secured land in Koh Phangan’s creative heartland — close enough to the village, far enough to breathe.

The gap

Creative people need focus, flow, and connection

Most spaces offer only one. A co-working space with no soul. A retreat with no work infrastructure. A community with no depth.

The people doing the most meaningful work — the founders, the artists, the builders — are making do with spaces that weren’t designed for them.

Overview

What we’re building

Accommodation

Treehouses and modular villas — designed for stays of weeks to months. Each unit is an investable asset.

Coworking

Deep-focus pods, shared desks, recording studio. Workspace designed for flow, not just wifi.

Wellness

Ice bath, traditional sauna, Japanese onsen. Recovery built into the daily rhythm, not a weekend add-on.

Café & Kitchen

A shared kitchen for residents and a café that serves both the campus and the neighbourhood.

Programs

Workshops, residencies, and retreats. Curated programming that attracts high-quality guests and generates revenue.

Roadmap

Three phases of growth

Phase 1 — Foundation

3 treehouses, wellness core (sauna, onsen, ice bath), early coworking. The seed — proving the model.

THB 10M · 2026–2027

Phase 2 — Expansion

Coworking expansion, modular villas, café, shared kitchen. Revenue diversification and scale.

THB 12M · 2027–2028

Phase 3 — Maturity

Makerspace, NāM Academy, off-site units. Full ecosystem. Self-sustaining creative campus.

THB 13M · 2028–2029

Total investment across all phases: THB 35M (~€972K)
