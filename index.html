What we're building

Casa NāM is a small, design-led residential village on Koh Phangan, created for people who want to live well on the island over time.

A residential rhythm

Private homes and shared spaces arranged to support the natural rhythm of island living.

Locally grounded architecture

Informed by local Thai architecture, shaped by climate and natural landscape, and designed to endure.

Space for everyday living

Casa NāM's Common Area includes shared hangout spaces, a pool, and a communal kitchen — designed for relaxed, everyday use.

The village is shaped to remain calm, coherent, and genuinely livable over time.

Why we're building Casa NāM

A response to how people increasingly want to live on Koh Phangan.

Living beyond the villa model

An alternative to isolated villas and yield-driven development — designed for everyday use, not short-term cycles.

Human-scale by design

Small enough for connection to emerge naturally, at one's own pace.

One place, many rhythms

Work, creativity, and daily life can coexist — making long-term island living viable.

Ownership options

Casa NāM offers a simple and flexible ownership structure, designed for long-term living.

Each home is a two-bedroom residence with two private ensuite bedrooms and a shared living level that includes kitchen, dining, and everyday living space.

The layout supports living together while maintaining clear personal boundaries.

This structure makes it possible to buy together and live independently, without compromising design quality, comfort, or long-term livability.

It's a practical way to access a well-designed home while sharing responsibility and cost.

In addition to the project's common areas, including a pool, hangout spaces, and a communal kitchen, each home includes its own shared living level for daily use.

Spaces are designed to adapt over time, allowing for more privacy if needs change.

Thoughtful design and quality materials, priced to remain genuinely accessible. By keeping the footprint compact, each home gains generous green space around it.

It's a practical way to access a well-designed home, with a balanced and flexible approach to ownership.

This is a place for people who are comfortable with independence and proximity existing at the same time.
